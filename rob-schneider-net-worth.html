What is Rob Schneider’s Net Worth and Salary?

Rob Schneider, the American actor, comedian, screenwriter, and director, has a net worth of $8 million. He has gained success through his career in films, television sitcoms, as a writer and performer for SNL, and his stand-up comedy routines. Schneider has collaborated with his friend Adam Sandler in several of Sandler’s biggest box office hits, including the commercially successful Grown Ups movies. Despite mixed reviews from critics, Schneider’s comedy films You Don’t Mess with the Zohan and The Animal performed well at the box office. Schneider’s Deuce Bigalow: Male Gigolo, which he wrote and starred in, was a surprise hit in 1999 and led to a sequel. While Schneider’s movie roles have not always been critically acclaimed, his popularity with audiences has contributed to his success in the entertainment industry.

Early Life and Background

Rob Schneider was born in San Francisco, California, in 1963. He was raised in Pacifica by his parents, Pilar and Marvin Schneider. His mother was a former kindergarten teacher and school board president, and his father was a real estate broker. Schneider’s heritage is mixed, with his father being Jewish, his mother Catholic, and his maternal grandmother from the Philippines. He attended Terra Nova High School and later San Francisco State University.

Schneider’s older brother, John, is a producer.

Rob Schneider’s Career Highlights

Rob Schneider began his career as a stand-up comedian in San Francisco, performing at local nightclubs and guest-starring on radio programs. He gained national exposure after appearing on HBO’s “13th Annual Young Comedians” special, which led to him joining the writing staff of “Saturday Night Live” in 1988. He quickly became a cast member and played memorable characters like “Tiny Elvis” and “The Richmeister”.

After leaving “SNL” in 1994, Schneider appeared in supporting roles in movies like “Surf Ninjas” and “The Beverly Hillbillies”. He also had a recurring part on the television series “Coach” and co-starred in the NBC sitcom “Men Behaving Badly”. Schneider went on to star in and co-write several successful comedies, including “Deuce Bigalow: Male Gigolo” and “The Hot Chick”. He also collaborated with fellow “SNL” alum Adam Sandler on multiple films, including “The Waterboy” and “Grown Ups”.

Outside of acting, Schneider has also worked as a director and released a comedy album titled “Registered Offender”. He has been a spokesperson for State Farm Insurance and the official celebrity spokesperson for the Taiwan Tourism Bureau and the Ten Ren Tea company in Taiwan.

Rob Schneider’s Personal Life and Philanthropy

Rob Schneider has two daughters – Elle King, born in 1989 to his former model partner London King, and two daughters with his current wife Patricia Azarcoya Arce, born in 2012 and 2016.

Schneider founded the Rob Schneider Music Foundation in 1996 to reintroduce music education programs in Pacifica’s elementary schools, which had been discontinued due to lack of funding. The foundation covered teacher salaries and provided funds for musical instruments and equipment.

Rob Schneider’s Real Estate Transactions

In 2003, Rob Schneider purchased a San Marino, California home for $1.922 million. Six years later, he listed the property for sale for $3.6 million. However, in 2012, he ultimately accepted $2.3 million for the home.

In 2004, Schneider sold his former LA home for $1.09 million.
