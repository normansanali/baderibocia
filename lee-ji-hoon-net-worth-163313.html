Lee Ji-hoon is an award-winning South Korean pop singer and actor. He was born in Seoul on March 27, 1979. He has released six solo albums between 1998 and 2004, including Rhythm Paradise, Love and Forever, Man, Eternity Friend and Special with..., and Trinity and The Classic in 2008. He began his career as part of a group before returning as a solo artist in 2004.

About

Award-winning pop singer and actor of film, television and the stage who released a number of solo albums titled Rhythm Paradise, Love and Forever, Man, Eternity Friend and Special with..., all within a six year span. After performing with a group, he returned as solo Artist in 2004 with an album titled Trinity. In 2008, he dropped The Classic.

Before Fame

His debut single "Why the Heaven" was released in October of 1996. The song was a part of his debut solo album titled Rhythm Paradise. He was a member of a trio called the "S" with Kangta and Shin Hye-sung.

Trivia

Film credits of his include Lovely Rivals and Wet Dreams 2. For TV, he starred in shows titled Wonderful Life, Hello! Miss, New Heart and You Are My Destiny. And on stage, he performed in productions of Lovers in Paris, Wicked, Elisabeth and more.

Family Life
