Gordon Hayward | NBA, Investments and Endorsements

NBA

Following his NCAA heroics, he was quickly signed as the 9th overall draft pick by the Utah Jazz.

Hayward soon evolved as an offensive player with the Jazz and would go on to 21.9 points, 5.4 rebounds, and 3.7 assists in the 2016-17 season.

The season would also be his last in Utah.

And Hayward didn’t need to wait long for his next move.

The Boston Celtics would soon come calling to acquire his services after being impressed with Hayward’s playoff run with the Jazz.

However, tragedy struck.

In the 2017-18 season, within six minutes of his first match for the Celtics, he would suffer a grueling ankle dislocation and would be ruled out for the rest of the season.

Hayward would then return in the subsequent season for the Celtics, and in the 2019-20 season, he would score a regular-season high of 39 points in a game against Cleveland.

In the 2020 off-season, he became a free agent and joined the Charlotte Hornets on another four-year deal.

Gordon Hayward | Career

Growing up in Indiana, Gordon attended Brownsburg High School, where he became a famed basketball prodigy.

He led Brownsburg to the Class 4A state championship and was rated as a three-star recruit joining Butlers University.

As a sophomore, he would lead them to the NCAA Divisions I Championship but would, unfortunately, lose to Duke University.

After two years with Butlers University, Hayward would then declare eligibility for the 2010 NBA Draft.
